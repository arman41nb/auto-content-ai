# Future Research Pack: What If Scenarios

## Known Facts
- These are speculative scenarios.
- Future topics work best when framed as possibilities, not predictions.
- Strong future posts usually connect a small change to a large consequence.

## Safe Claims
- A what-if scenario can explore possible consequences without claiming certainty.
- Future convenience may create hidden costs.
- Survival questions can make abstract futures feel concrete.
- Social, environmental, and daily-life impacts are often more relatable than pure technology.

## Avoided Claims
- Do not present fictional outcomes as guaranteed facts.
- Do not imply a future event is inevitable without evidence.
- Avoid pretending invented technology already exists.
- Avoid precise timelines unless sourced elsewhere.

## Notes For Fact Caution
- Use clear "what if" framing.
- Use "could", "might", and "possible" for speculative outcomes.
- Keep scenarios internally consistent and grounded in daily human experience.

## Recommended Angles
- Small change, massive consequence.
- Future convenience with hidden cost.
- Survival question.
- The future looks clean, but may feel complicated.
