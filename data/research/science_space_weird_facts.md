# Science Research Pack: Space Weird Facts

## Known Facts
- Space facts should be framed carefully.
- Exoplanets, neutron stars, black holes, and extreme environments are strong visual topics.
- Some exoplanets may have extreme weather unlike anything on Earth.
- Neutron stars are incredibly dense remnants of massive stars.
- Black holes can be shown with accretion disks when matter surrounds them.

## Safe Claims
- Space is beautiful, but many environments are violent or hostile.
- Scientists think some exoplanets may have extreme winds, temperatures, or unusual atmospheric chemistry.
- Tiny cosmic objects can involve extreme density or gravity.
- Human intuition often fails at space scales.

## Avoided Claims
- Avoid exaggerating uncertain science as absolute.
- Avoid saying speculative weather is confirmed everywhere.
- Avoid impossible visuals unless clearly framed as speculative.
- Avoid turning hypotheses into guaranteed facts.

## Notes For Fact Caution
- Use "may", "could", or "scientists think" for uncertain claims.
- Keep black hole, neutron star, and exoplanet claims broad unless separately sourced.
- Visual metaphors are acceptable when labeled through prompt context as speculative.

## Recommended Angles
- Space is beautiful but violent.
- Some planets may have weather that feels impossible.
- Tiny objects can have extreme density.
- The universe is stranger when you leave textbook scale.

## Visual Ideas
- violent exoplanet atmosphere
- neutron star
- black hole accretion disk
- glass-like rain as a speculative visual
- lonely spacecraft near a huge planet
