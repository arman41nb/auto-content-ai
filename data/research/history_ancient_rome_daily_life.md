# History Research Pack: Ancient Rome Daily Life

## Known Facts
- Many ordinary urban Romans lived in apartment blocks called insulae.
- Roman streets could be crowded, noisy, smoky, and unsanitary.
- Ordinary people did not live like emperors.
- Elite banquets existed, but ordinary diets were simpler.
- Public baths were a major part of daily social life.
- Rome looked grand in monuments, but daily life could be harsh.

## Safe Claims
- Daily life in Rome varied sharply by wealth, legal status, gender, and location.
- Urban Romans often experienced crowding, street noise, smoke, and public social spaces.
- Simple foods such as bread, grains, legumes, olives, and oil were common for many non-elite people.
- Bathhouses, markets, streets, and apartment blocks are useful daily-life settings.

## Avoided Claims
- Avoid exact claims about "most people" unless phrased carefully.
- Avoid saying every Roman lived in poverty.
- Avoid focusing on elite scandals unless directly related to daily life.
- Avoid treating movie-style imperial luxury as normal daily life.

## Notes For Fact Caution
- Use "many ordinary urban Romans" instead of "all Romans."
- Distinguish elite villas and banquets from ordinary urban living.
- Avoid exact work-hour claims unless sourced elsewhere.

## Recommended Angles
- Rome looked glorious from far away, rough up close.
- You would notice noise, smell, smoke, and crowding first.
- Ordinary Romans did not live like movie emperors.
- The empire's daily reality was less marble, more cramped streets.
